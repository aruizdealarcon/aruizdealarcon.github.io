Alberto Ruiz–de–Alarcón

I am Assistant Professor at the Department of Mathematics of CUNEF Universidad since January 2025. Previously, I held a postdoctoral position at Universidad Complutense de Madrid and, before that, at Eberhard Karls Universität Tübingen. I obtained my PhD in Mathematics from Universidad Complutense de Madrid, under the supervision of David Pérez-García and András Molnár, supported by an FPI Severo Ochoa fellowship at the ICMAT-CSIC.

I work at the interface between mathematics and theoretical quantum physics. I focus on understanding quantum many-body systems using ideas from quantum information theory. I am particularly interested in the structure of tensor network states and their symmetries, how to classify different quantum phases of matter, and how topological order appears in these systems. I also study the behavior of quantum thermal states, how quantum systems evolve in open environments, and the mathematical frameworks behind all this—like quantum groupoids and fusion categories.

You can also find me on Google Scholar, ORCiD, WoS and arXiv.
