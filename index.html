I am interested in several aspects of the interplay between mathematics and theoretical quantum physics. These include analytical results on strongly correlated quantum many-body systems, such as the structure of tensor networks states and symmetries, the different phase classification programmes, the description of topological order phenomena, the structure and properties of quantum thermal states, and the theory of quantum groupoids, fusion categories and operator algebras.

As of February 2024, I have returned to the group as a postdoctoral researcher within the Quantum Spain project, with the purpose of investigating the internal symmetries and dissipative process convergence in quantum many-body and quantum-inspired systems related to tensor networks.

Previously, I held postdoctoral fellow position at the University of Tübingen in Germany under the supervision of Ángela Capel and Stefan Stefan Teufel, as part of the SFB TRR 352 project, focusing on the analysis of the response to local perturbations of quantum thermal states.

I started my academic education with a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from the Complutense University of Madrid, followed by a Master's degree in Advanced Mathematics at the same university, with final degree thesis about measure and metric space theory. Afterwards, I began my doctoral studies at the same university under the supervision of David Pérez García and András Molnár, supported by a Severo Ochoa Research Personnel Training Contract (FPI-SO) at the Institute of Mathematical Sciences (ICMAT-CSIC). During my studies I carried out a four-month stay in the group of Norbert Schuch at the University of Vienna. I defended my thesis in January 2023, titled "Weak Hopf algebras, Matrix Product Operators and the classification of quantum phases of matter".

You can also find me on my Google Scholar, ORCiD, arXiv and institutional pages.
